Resume tips for a cleaner, stronger application

A good resume is not just about design. It also needs clear wording, useful structure, and bullet points that explain your value quickly. These tips are meant to be practical, not generic.

1. Keep the layout simple

A clean resume is easier for recruiters to scan and easier for ATS systems to parse. Avoid visual clutter and keep the structure obvious.

2. Write bullet points with action and outcome

Good bullet points show what you did, how you did it, and what changed because of your work. Even small results make your experience easier to understand.

3. Use clear section headings

Standard headings like Experience, Education, Skills, and Projects help both recruiters and applicant tracking systems understand your resume faster.

4. Focus on relevant information

Keep the achievements, tools, and experience most relevant to the role near the top. Your resume should guide the reader to your fit quickly.

5. Cut weak filler words

Phrases like “responsible for” and “helped with” often hide your contribution. Use more direct language that shows ownership and action.

6. Tailor the resume to the role

You do not need a totally different resume every time, but you should adjust the summary, skills, and strongest bullets to fit the job target.

7. Use metrics when they help

Numbers make impact easier to trust. Include metrics such as time saved, revenue influenced, output volume, accuracy, growth, or user reach when relevant.

8. Rewrite unclear wording

If your resume sounds vague, repetitive, or passive, use AI support to make the language clearer, more direct, and more professional without changing the facts.
