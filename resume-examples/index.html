Resume examples that actually show what to write

These examples are not meant to be copied word for word. They show the kind of content that works well inside a clean ATS-friendly format: clear responsibilities, relevant skills, and outcomes that are easy to scan.

Student

Student resume example

Focus on potential, projects, coursework, and signs that you take initiative.

  • Lead with education, expected graduation date, and relevant courses
  • Add projects, student teams, volunteering, or part-time work
  • Show tools you used, what you built, and what the outcome was
  • Keep bullet points specific instead of writing long general statements
Graduate

Graduate resume example

Show that your academic background connects clearly to entry-level work.

  • Use a short summary only if it adds context to your target role
  • Highlight internships, thesis work, labs, research, or client projects
  • Emphasize tools, analysis, collaboration, and measurable results
  • Move your strongest achievements above less relevant older experience
Software

Software resume example

Recruiters want to see technologies, scope, and impact without digging.

  • Name the stack, your contribution, and what improved because of your work
  • Include projects with users, performance gains, reliability, or automation wins
  • Separate core languages, frameworks, and tools in a clean skills section
  • Replace vague bullets like “worked on app” with implementation details
Marketing

Marketing resume example

Use numbers and business context so your contribution feels concrete.

  • Show campaign results, content performance, conversions, or audience growth
  • Make channel ownership clear: email, paid social, SEO, events, or content
  • Keep each bullet tied to a result, not just a task list
  • Include tools only when they support the story of your experience
Professional

Experienced professional example

Prioritize recent and relevant achievements over a long history dump.

  • Start with your strongest recent role and most relevant responsibilities
  • Cut older details that no longer help you get interviews
  • Use stronger verbs and outcomes to show leadership or ownership
  • Keep formatting simple so the reader finds your best points fast

How AI should improve an example

Good AI editing makes content clearer. It should not invent experience.

  • Rewrite weak bullets into clearer action + outcome statements
  • Remove repeated wording and generic phrases
  • Reorder sections so the most relevant content appears first
  • Keep the facts true while making the language stronger
