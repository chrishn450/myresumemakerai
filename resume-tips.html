Resume tips

Most resumes do not fail because the person lacks experience. They fail because the content is vague, repetitive, or hard to scan. Small changes in wording and structure can make a big difference.

Use stronger bullet points

Replace generic phrases with actions and outcomes. Focus on what you improved, built, supported, analyzed, or delivered.

Keep the summary short

A summary should quickly explain who you are, what you do, and where you add value. Avoid long paragraphs with filler language.

Tailor when possible

Matching the job description helps both recruiters and ATS systems understand your fit more clearly.

Start with what you already have

If you already have an old CV, upload it and improve it with AI instead of rebuilding everything from scratch.
